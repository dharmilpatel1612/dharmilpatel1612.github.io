Important Habits for Good Eye Health

Getting into a routine for eye care can be difficult. Unlike your teeth, which can be kept clean with the well-known brushing and flossing routine, your eye-care routine can be, well, difficult to see.

In order to ensure that your eyes remain in good health, here are five things you should do every day.

Diet. A nutritious diet is critical for good eye health. Eating antioxidant-rich vegetables, particularly dark, leafy greens like kale or spinach, is beneficial to your eyes. These dark, leafy greens are high in two antioxidants that help protect your eyes from light: lutein and zeaxanthin. Fish high in Omega-3 fatty acids, such as salmon and tuna, are also good for your eyes. These fish's Omega-3 fatty acids can help prevent dry eyes, cataracts, and even macular degeneration. Furthermore, eating a healthy diet will help you avoid diseases such as diabetes, which can lead to eye complications.

You should not smoke. We all know that smoking is bad for your lungs, but it's also bad for your eyes. According to the National Eye Institute, smokers are more likely to develop cataracts, macular degeneration, glaucoma, and optic nerve damage. It simply isn't worth the risk.

Put on your sunglasses. Sunglasses are more than just a summer fashion accessory. They assist in shielding your eyes from the Sun's harmful UV-A and UV-B rays. While you can apply sunscreen to protect your skin, sunglasses are required to protect your eyes. There are numerous sunglasses options available, and if you wear glasses, you can even have prescription sunglasses made at most vision retailers. However, regardless of where you buy your sunglasses or what style you choose, make sure they are UV-A and UV-B rated.

Blue-light blocking glasses and on-the-job safety glasses. If you work in construction, manufacturing, or any other industry where your eyes may be exposed to hazardous materials, you should invest in a good pair of safety glasses. To provide the best protection, these glasses should be made of poly-carbonate, which does not shatter like glass, and should completely cover your eyes, including the sides. Even if you work in an office, prolonged contact with a computer screen can be hazardous to your eyes. Blue-light blocking glasses can protect your eyes from long computer sessions and even help prevent tension headaches.

Maintain your contacts. If you wear contact lenses, keep them clean. Wearing dirty contacts can cause infections and even long-term vision damage. The best way to avoid contact-related infections is to wash your lenses thoroughly, change them as directed by your optometrist, and always wash your hands before handling your contacts.

There are no guarantees, but by adopting these five behaviours, you can help maintain the health of your eyes for years to come!
